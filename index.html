About Beau

A Yooper. A Fighter. Your Neighbor.

Beau served 6 years as State Representative for the 108th District (2017-2022) and then 3 years as legislative staff serving the entire 38th Senate District and the State of Michigan — including Chippewa and Mackinac counties. He's a lifelong U.P. resident who went to law school so he could actually write laws that work.

When the COVID lockdowns hit, Beau stood up for the U.P. He defended Upper Peninsula families and businesses against unconstitutional restrictions from the very beginning — and he never backed down.

Beau Fought to Lower Your Car Insurance

Michigan had the most expensive car insurance in the nation. Beau helped change that.

For over 45 years, Michigan's broken no-fault insurance system forced drivers to pay the highest premiums in the country — with mandatory unlimited lifetime coverage that no other state required. U.P. families were paying thousands more per year than neighbors in Wisconsin.

Beau served on the House Select Committee on Reducing Car Insurance Rates and voted for the landmark 2019 reform that finally gave Michigan drivers a choice in coverage levels, cracked down on fraud, and delivered real rate relief.

The result? A $400 refund per insured vehicle and permanent rate reductions for families across the Upper Peninsula.

"This historic reform has been over 45 years in the making, and I'm honored to deliver real rate savings to the people who deserve it the most — Michigan drivers."

— Beau LaFave

Where Beau Stands

Clear positions. No waffling. Real solutions for the U.P.

Second Amendment Rights

Gretchen Whitmer has made it clear she intends to disarm Yoopers. Beau open-carried at the State of the State address to tell her she could start with him. He doesn't just vote pro-2A — he lives it. In the State Senate, Beau will defend against every attempt to infringe on your right to keep and bear arms.

Property Rights & Limited Government

Beau introduced the warrant requirement bill for the DNR so they have to operate like every other law enforcement agency. For years, he's taken on agencies that treat Yoopers' land like their own personal playground. He opposed every fee increase on park passports, trail permits, and hunting/fishing licenses. Your property is yours, not the state's.

Energy Independence

LaFave is frequently critiqued for his stances on energy, but notes that critics quickly abandon their principles when it's cold outside and they use natural gas to heat their homes. Radical leftists focused on Detroit and Grand Rapids advance legislation that would shut down Line 5, leading to exorbitant energy costs that are already high in the U.P. The U.P. needs one of their own to defend against this. Beau sponsored the "Build the Tunnel" legislation that would connect the Upper and Lower Peninsulas and make Canada pay for it.

Holding the Executive Accountable

Beau fought Governor Whitmer every step of the way during COVID restrictions — he didn't wait for permission. He introduced legislation to end the lockdowns, sued the Governor and won, made public call outs, and when she still wouldn't give up the ghost, he filed articles of impeachment. It took a while, but we eventually got our republic back.

U.P. Economy

The Upper Peninsula's economy depends on mining, timber, tourism, and manufacturing. Beau will defend these industries from overregulation and environmental extremism. He'll work to keep energy affordable and ensure that the U.P. isn't left behind while Lansing focuses on Detroit and Grand Rapids.
