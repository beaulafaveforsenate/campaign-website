Where Beau Stands

Clear positions. No waffling. Real solutions for the U.P.

Second Amendment Rights

Beau doesn't just vote pro-2A. He lives it. He famously open-carried at the State of the State address to send a clear message: our constitutional rights are not up for debate. In the State Senate, Beau will defend against every attempt to infringe on your right to keep and bear arms.

Property Rights

For years, Beau has taken on the DNR and other agencies that treat Yoopers' land like their own personal playground. He stood up against ATV fee increases, opposed the Recreation Passport mandate, and passed legislation forcing government agencies to pay court costs when they lose. Your property is yours, not the state's.

Limited Government

Government should serve the people, not rule over them. When Governor Whitmer used COVID as an excuse to seize unprecedented power, Beau didn't wait for permission. He introduced articles of impeachment. That's the kind of bold leadership the U.P. needs in Lansing.

U.P. Economy

The Upper Peninsula's economy depends on mining, timber, tourism, and manufacturing. Beau will defend these industries from overregulation and environmental extremism. He'll work to keep energy affordable and ensure that the U.P. isn't left behind while Lansing focuses on Detroit and Grand Rapids.

Holding the Executive Accountable

Beau was one of the few legislators willing to take on a Governor abusing emergency powers. While others sat on their hands, Beau filed articles of impeachment against Gretchen Whitmer. He believes in separation of powers and will never let the executive branch run roughshod over the legislature or the people.

Energy Independence

The U.P. is facing an energy crisis that threatens jobs at Cleveland Cliffs and throughout the region. Beau will protect our energy supply and keep it reliable and affordable. That means defending existing power sources while Lansing's green energy mandates threaten to leave the U.P. in the dark.
